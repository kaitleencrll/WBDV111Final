🚑 Critical First Aid Protocols

🩸 Hemorrhage & Profuse Bleeding Control

Clinical Assessment: Identify if the blood is bright red/spurting (arterial) or dark/oozing (venous).

  • Immediate Action: Apply firm, continuous pressure with a sterile dressing or the cleanest cloth available.
  • Secondary Layer: If blood soaks through, do not remove the first layer as it disrupts clot formation. Add more padding on top.
  • Elevation: If possible, keep the wounded area above the level of the heart.
  • Tourniquet Warning: Only use as a last resort for life-threatening limb bleeding.
🤢 Toxic Ingestion & Chemical Exposure

Clinical Assessment: Look for hypersalivation, tremors, vomiting, or seizures.

  • Contact Data: Immediately identify the product name and active ingredients.
  • Skin Exposure: Flush area with lukewarm water and mild dish soap for 20 minutes.
  • Oral Ingestion: 🚫 NEVER induce vomiting for corrosive substances (bleach/acids).
  • Transport: Bring packaging or vomit sample to the vet.
🌡️ Heatstroke (Hyperthermia) Response

Clinical Assessment: Rectal temperature above 104°F, brick-red gums, thick ropey saliva.

  • Evacuation: Move to a ventilated, shaded area immediately.
  • Cooling Method: Use room-temperature water (not ice) on footpads, groin, and underarms.
  • Circulation: Use an electric fan while the pet is damp.
  • Monitoring: Stop cooling once temperature reaches 103°F.
🦴 Fracture Stabilization & Trauma Transport

Clinical Assessment: Visible deformity, inability to bear weight, or localized swelling.

  • Safety First: Use a soft muzzle or wrap-around towel before handling.
  • Splinting Policy: Do not attempt to "set" the bone. Cover protruding bones with damp sterile cloth.
  • Stretcher Use: Slide a flat, rigid board under the pet.
  • Confinement: Secure in a small crate for transport.
